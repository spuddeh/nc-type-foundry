Two systems, both canonical: the type runs 26/17 strokes with 20×14 chamfers on bends; the monogram runs uniform 22.5 strokes with everything at 45°, which is also the system the favicon Z was designed to suit.

Monogram

Monogram remaster, same process as the letters

The bmp trace (solid) with the pure rebuild over it (cyan outline). Uniform 22.5-unit strokes, the N diagonal at exactly 45° with a 37-unit horizontal cross-section, the slash bar a 45°-cut parallelogram. Now baked into logo v3 alongside the pure lettering.

0 to 9, tabular on a 110 cell

Punctuation

Core marks

Dots are 20×17 blocks; the comma and semicolon tails are the only strokes allowed below the baseline. Slashes carry the Z diagonal at 17 perpendicular. Parens chamfer their outer bends; brackets stay square. The percent rings are the one deliberate off-system reduction. Tier 0 site additions: underscore and chevrons (the terminal prompt), bullet (station IDs), and the eddies pair (the euro is a C-frame with two square-junction crossbars; the dollar runs its stem through the S counters to stay inside the cell).

Notes

  • Two canonical systems. The favicon Z belongs to the monogram system (uniform strokes, 45° cuts), the font Z to the type system (26/17, 20×14). Neither should be "harmonised" to the other.
  • Original traces are preserved in svg-original-trace/ beside the font files; logo v2 remains the immutable reference.
  • Corp marks: the enclosed © ® ℗ rings hold literal scaled instances of the family C, R and P; ™ is scaled T and M. Nothing remains deferred; the extended set is complete.
